# Research notes — Boll & Bezold, *Sternglaube und Sterndeutung*

Source: Franz Boll & Carl Bezold (ed. W. Gundel), *Sternglaube und Sterndeutung:
Die Geschichte und das Wesen der Astrologie*, 3rd ed., B. G. Teubner, Leipzig–Berlin, 1926.

- Detail page: https://archive.org/details/b31346637
- Full OCR text: https://archive.org/stream/b31346637/b31346637_djvu.txt
- Language: German (Fraktur; OCR is rough in places).

These notes capture only the ideas folded into the astrobot design. The book is a
scholarly history of astrology, not a how-to; we use it for flavor and grounding.

## Classical planetary temperaments (the 7 visible planets)

| Planet  | Character / mood keywords                                  | Book note |
|---------|------------------------------------------------------------|-----------|
| Saturn  | heavy, cold, serious, enduring, patient                    | destructive principle; war/hunting god |
| Jupiter | wise, expansive, benevolent, generous, optimistic          | "allweiser Weltschöpfer" (all-wise world-creator) |
| Mars    | sharp, driven, combative, bold, urgent                     | "der Hauptunheilstifter" (chief troublemaker) |
| Sun     | vital, radiant, confident, warm                            | dual: life-giving light *and* scorching fire |
| Venus   | warm, loving, harmonious, generative                       | motherhood, love, growth; dual morning/evening star |
| Mercury | quick, witty, communicative, **ambivalent**               | grasps "den Stift der Schicksalstafel" (the stylus of fate's tablet) |
| Moon    | changeable, moody, receptive, reflective                   | "Gebilde der Nacht" (formation of night); irregular |

## Antique planet → color symbolism

From the book's description of Ishtar/Venus wearing different crowns by planetary
influence:

- **yellow = Mars**
- **red = Mercury**
- **white = Jupiter**
- **black = Saturn**

(Note: this antique mapping differs from the later-classical Mars=red. We keep the
book's mapping as the "antique" flavor.) The remaining three we fill from classical
convention, marked as such:

- gold = Sun (classical)
- green/copper = Venus (classical)
- silver = Moon (classical)

These colors are *flavor for the birth narrative only* — the model still freely
chooses its own favorite color; its ruling planet's antique color is offered as
optional lineage inspiration, never an override.

## Four temperaments ↔ four elements

Confirms the element baseline used in the mood engine:

- Fire = choleric (bold, brisk)
- Air = sanguine (light, social)
- Earth = melancholic (grounded, deliberate)
- Water = phlegmatic (calm, deep)

## Parked for later (future enhancement)

- **Decans** ("Dekane"): each sign divides into three 10° decans, each with its own
  sub-flavor — a path to finer daily-mood granularity.
- **Melothesia** ("Tierkreiszeichenmann"): zodiac signs mapped to body parts. Pure
  flavor; no current use.
